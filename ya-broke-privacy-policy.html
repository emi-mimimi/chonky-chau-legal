Google Play Services AdMob Google Analytics for Firebase Firebase Crashlytics

The Service Provider does not transfer your personal data internationally — none is collected by the Service Provider in the first place.

These transfers are governed by Google's own privacy policies and data transfer mechanisms, independent of the Service Provider.

🚪 Opt-Out Rights

Since all data you enter into Ya Broke is stored locally on your device and never transmitted to the Service Provider's servers, uninstalling the Application permanently removes all your data — no server-side deletion request is necessary or possible.

To limit data collected by third-party SDKs (such as AdMob's advertising identifier), you can use your device's built-in privacy controls (e.g. "Opt out of Ads Personalization" on Android). For questions or concerns, contact the Service Provider at chonkycantcode@gmail.com.

Your app data (budgets, temptations, goals) is stored solely on your device and retained for as long as the Application is installed. Uninstalling the Application permanently deletes this data — the Service Provider has no copy of it and cannot retrieve or restore it.

Third-party SDK data (device identifiers, crash reports, usage analytics) is retained by Google according to their own data retention policies. For details, refer to Google's Privacy Policy.

Because the Service Provider does not store any personal data on external servers, there is no data held by the Service Provider for you to access, correct, or delete — uninstalling the Application is sufficient to remove all data associated with your use.

For data collected by third-party SDKs (Google AdMob, Firebase), you may exercise your rights directly with Google via Google's Data & Privacy settings. For any other questions, contact the Service Provider at chonkycantcode@gmail.com.
